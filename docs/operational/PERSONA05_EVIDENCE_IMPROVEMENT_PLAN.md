# Persona 05 Evidence Improvement Plan

## Candidate Definition

- `core_job_or_situation`: An analyst or operations owner is trying to produce a presentation-ready report, table, filter view, or stakeholder-facing dashboard cut under delivery pressure.
- `pain`: The primary BI tool can hold the data but cannot easily produce the final shape, interaction, formatting, or cross-table behavior needed, so the analyst gets stuck in last-mile report construction.
- `desired_output`: A presentation-ready, stakeholder-usable dashboard/table/view that can be delivered without manual workaround or tool-specific contortions.
- `how_it_differs_from_persona_01`: persona_01 is broader manual reporting burden and recurring export/spreadsheet rework. persona_05 is narrower: manual reporting is present, but the defining pain is last-mile output construction blocked by tool limitations and deadline pressure.
- `how_it_differs_from_persona_03`: persona_03 is the cleaner generic tool-limitation workaround persona. persona_05 is more output-oriented: the user is not just blocked by the tool generally, but specifically while trying to produce a presentable report, table, or stakeholder-ready view.
- `why_separate_persona`: It captures a repeatable last-mile delivery pattern that is not fully explained by spreadsheet/export burden alone and not fully explained by generic tool-limitation workaround alone.

## Persona 05 vs Persona 01

- `overlapping_signals`:
  - manual_reporting as the dominant primary bottleneck
  - report creation and stakeholder delivery context
  - some presentation_ready_output_need overlap
- `differentiating_signals`:
  - persona_05 rows more often include explicit tool_limitation_workaround
  - persona_05 rows cluster around last-mile output construction, layout, filtering, formatting, or view behavior
  - persona_01 has much stronger recurring_export_work and spreadsheet_rework signatures
- `classification`: not just a tail of persona_01, but currently too blurry and under-grounded to stand alone cleanly

## Persona 05 vs Persona 03

- `overlapping_signals`:
  - tool_limitation_workaround is a recurring signature
  - many rows involve product features that cannot express the needed output behavior
- `differentiating_signals`:
  - persona_05 is usually manual-reporting-led rather than pure tool-limitation-led
  - persona_05 adds presentation_ready_output_need and reporting_deadline_pressure much more often
  - persona_03 is a cleaner generic tool-workaround persona without the same output-delivery emphasis
- `classification`: not just a tail of persona_03, but a nearby sibling pattern that still needs cleaner narrative boundaries
